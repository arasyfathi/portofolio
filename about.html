About Me

I'm an Electrical Engineering student who enjoys building physical and digital systems that genuinely solve real-world problems. Most of my work sits at the intersection of the Internet of Things (IoT), hardware automation, and artificial intelligence. I like taking ideas from the first concept all the way to a functional prototype—whether that means designing precise circuit logic, programming microcontrollers, or structuring reliable real-time web dashboards. I focus heavily on tangible results. In past projects, I've built automated waste sorting systems, AI image classifiers, and real-time environmental monitoring tools that streamline data acquisition. My goal is always the same: to create smart, scalable, and efficient engineering solutions that make an impact.

I am an engineer who bridges the gap between physical hardware and intelligent software. With hands-on experience building IoT-powered monitoring systems, precise automated mechanisms, and AI-driven web applications, I specialize in solving complex engineering challenges while delivering robust and efficient technical systems.

My approach combines rigorous hardware design with modern software integration. Whether it's implementing precise sensor readings with MPU-6050 and DHT22, developing automation control logic using PLCs, or architecting real-time monitoring dashboards with ESP32 and Flask servers, I consistently deliver reliable prototypes that merge mechanical actuation with intelligent data processing.

What sets me apart is my ability to work across both the technical stack and team dynamics. Beyond programming in C and Python, deploying AI models, and wiring circuits, I have a strong background in Public Relations and R&D. I don't just write code or build hardware—I communicate complex engineering concepts effectively, build strategic organizational partnerships, and lead projects that drive meaningful innovation.
