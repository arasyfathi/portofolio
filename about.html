About Me

Who I am & what I do.

I’m an Electrical Engineering student who enjoys building physical and digital systems that genuinely solve real-world problems. Most of my work sits at the intersection of the Internet of Things (IoT), hardware automation, and artificial intelligence.

I’m an engineer who bridges the gap between physical hardware and intelligent software. With hands-on experience building IoT-powered monitoring systems, precise automated mechanisms, and AI-driven web applications, I specialize in solving complex engineering challenges while delivering robust and efficient technical systems.

My approach combines rigorous hardware design with modern software integration. Whether it’s implementing precise sensor readings with MPU-6050 and DHT22, developing automation control logic using PLCs, or architecting real-time monitoring dashboards with ESP32 and Blynk — I consistently deliver reliable prototypes that merge mechanical actuation with intelligent data processing.

I trained a YOLOv8 object detection model for the SmartWaste project to automate waste classification, deploying it through a Flask web interface. I also built IoT control systems managed through Blynk.Console and Blynk.cloud, enabling real-time hardware monitoring and remote actuation directly from a dashboard.
